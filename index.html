Summary

(As well as we can give it)

Chunk One (1-7)

58 years ago, Dominis, a photographer from Los Angeles California, captured a moment of protest during the Summer Olympics of 1968. That picture became an important picture for the history of the black power movement

Chunk Two (8-12)

The Protest was dubbed "A cry for freedom", the protest, the defiance against the Olympic Coordinators, which helped show the backbone for fighting injustices within sports

Chunk Three (13-22)

The act of protest is described in detail, from nothing slowly to the podium, to raising their fists in the air, the athletes are unshaken by the crowd.

Chunk Four (23-30)

The two athletes describe their motivations behind the protest act, stating that no hatred for America has been shown

Chunk 5 (31-34)

Despite initially being outcasted for protesting; Those who protested stood for what they believed in, which shows their resolve and honor.

Article Viewpoint

DeNeen L. Brown's View

DeNeen believes that Athletes deserve the right to protest, and should not have consequences for speaking out because even though they may face consequences they still will protest, and because it is morally correct for them to protest.

Counter Viewpoint

Reader View

Athletes do not deserve the right to protest because it could ruin their career, and because sports are intended for entertainment and not for political changes.
